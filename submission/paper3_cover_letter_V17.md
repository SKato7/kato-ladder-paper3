Dear Editors,

Please consider the manuscript, "After von Foerster: Seventy Thousand Years of Human Coordination on the Kato Ladder, and a Civilizational Ontology of Six Realized Phase Transitions," for publication as an Article in Nature Human Behaviour. This manuscript reorganizes the last roughly 70,000 years of human population history as a discrete civilizational ontology of six realized phase transitions on a common pairwise backbone, proves a conditional necessity theorem for the next-rung continuation out of the present regime, and calibrates a falsifiable forecast window for that continuation to [2028.4, 2034.4] CE with ±3 yr canonical robustness. The substrate on which the prospective next rung would be realized is explicitly left as an open question for companion work, so the contribution of this paper is structural and forecastable rather than technology-specific.

This submission is unusually timely. Von Foerster, Mora, and Amiot's 1960 population singularity date—November 13, 2026—is now close enough that the underlying question has become newly concrete: why does the singularity not arrive, even though the long-run pairwise engine of human population growth remains visible in the record? Our answer is that the active ceiling changes, and that at H=6 the continuation problem itself becomes structural rather than descriptive.

The manuscript makes three claims that I believe are directly relevant to NHB readers.

First, it proves a conditional necessity theorem for the next-rung continuation out of the present regime, by exhaustion of the three admissible non-transition routes. Under the V_F-exogeneity theorem, broad endogenous closures V_F = f(N, Ω) are knife-edge unstable (κ ≈ 1.19 × 10⁵) and generically overproduce collapse events, so long-run reset dynamics require an exogenous ceiling envelope. Under the rational survival form η(H) = L_exo / (L_exo + τ_endo(H)), that same envelope implies a finite H=6 residence lifetime,

    L_civ = L_exo · (1 − η(H=6)) / η(H=6),

because η(H=6) < 1. A frontier trajectory that fails to reach the next rung must belong to exactly one of three reduced-form routes: Route A (a renewed thresholded collapse of the N/V_F type), Route B (indefinite same-rung stall misread from the post-1963 slowdown), or Route C (genuine retrogression that destroys already achieved relay depth). The manuscript shows that, under the retained Paper 3 source reading, each of the three routes is excluded as the operative branch on the historical baseline. By exhaustion, the surviving forward continuation is the next-rung transition H=6→7, in the reduced-form sense. This changes the next-rung claim from a descriptive possibility into an exhaustion-based conditional necessity, and it identifies three clean falsifiers rather than a single mood of inevitability.

Second, it establishes a necessary-condition theorem on H=7 carrier properties without committing to substrate existence. The biological primitives of the Humanness theorem—C₁ dyadic cooperation, C₂ cumulative intergenerational inheritance, and C₃ external symbolic memory—are proved self-contained as necessary conditions for native H ≥ 3 coordination, with pairwise logical independence against three explicit counter-model societies. As a consequence, any candidate carrier that would realize the next rung must satisfy the functional analogs of C₁, C₂, and C₃. Whether a non-biological carrier with those analogs actually exists is an existence-side question that this manuscript deliberately does not adjudicate. A companion Paper 5, in preparation, supplies the substrate-neutral existence side. This split lets the present manuscript deliver a precise, source-locked "necessary-condition" result without entangling the historical reconstruction with speculative substrate claims.

Third, it presents a falsifiable forecast window rather than a mood of technological speculation. The revised SI shows that Eq.(2) lives on a rank-2 parameter manifold, not an unconstrained three-parameter box, and that the headline date 2031.4 CE remains centered inside a source-supported residual-bootstrap interval of [2028.4, 2034.4] CE. The same SI now consolidates a seven-axis falsifier battery—matrix, hum, eq2, omega, eta, a1b, and forecast—so the framework can fail cleanly at the level of backbone, theorem, timing law, or date forecast. In the forward-looking decade, the next-rung claim is independently testable through three channels: failure of the semilog compression law itself, realization of a transition outside the date window, or continued failure of carrier-replacement proxy thresholds required to count a genuine handoff.

I believe the paper fits Nature Human Behaviour because its central object is collective human coordination under changing social and behavioural constraints. Fertility decline, cumulative cultural inheritance, external memory, institutional complexity, and the transition toward hybrid human-computational coordination are treated here not as separate topics but as linked phases of one long-run behavioural trajectory. In this revised form, the manuscript does more than organize roughly 70,000 years into six coordination regimes: it argues that those regimes unfold under successive ceiling replacement, that the present regime is non-terminal, and that the next-rung continuation problem is theoretically bounded and near-term falsifiable.

The revised manuscript is also explicit about proof burden. Paper 3 carries the self-contained burden for V_F-exogeneity, the K_P3 pre-H=3 kernel, the Humanness theorem (including pairwise logical independence of C₁, C₂, C₃ against three explicit counter-model societies), the rational survival form of η(H), the Kato-ladder characterization across the six realized rungs, and the Route A/B/C exhaustion leading to the conditional next-rung theorem. It leans on companion work only for three narrower items: substrate-neutral existence on non-biological carriers (Paper 5, in preparation), numerical calibration of η(H=6) beyond the rational form retained self-contained (Paper 4), and Kramers-rate numerics for reset probabilities (Paper 6). I believe this explicit dependency structure strengthens the submission because it makes the conditional necessity claim auditable and it isolates the substrate-existence question as separate future work rather than an implicit assumption.

A companion Paper 5, in preparation, broadens the argument from the human historical record to a substrate-neutral theory of H-formation and addresses the existence side of the H=7 carrier question. Paper 3 is the human and civilizational anchor of that broader programme: it establishes the necessary-condition structure of the next-rung transition self-contained, while the companion paper takes up the question of whether any non-biological carrier can realize the required C₁/C₂/C₃ analogs.

The framework remains explicitly falsifiable. The Neanderthal row is still predeclared falsifiable in the strongest sense: discovery of a genuine Neanderthal archive or writing system would directly overturn the published C₃ assignment and reclassify that row from (C₁=✓, C₂=△, C₃=×) to H ≥ 3. Likewise, failure of the forecast window would reject the forward prediction without erasing the historical reconstruction, which is precisely why this letter separates core, support, and forecast claims.
